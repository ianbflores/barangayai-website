Common Questions

Questions from Barangay Officials

Answers to what LGUs ask us most — about cost, technical requirements, and what partnership really means.

Does our barangay need to be technically advanced to partner with BarangayAI?
+
No — we specifically designed BarangayAI for communities without existing digital infrastructure. You need nothing more than an internet connection and willing community members. We handle all technical setup, and our training programs are built for non-technical users.
How much does the partnership cost?
+
Costs vary by partnership tier and program scope. Pilot Barangay partnerships are fully subsidized through our donor and support programs — qualifying barangays pay nothing. Municipal and provincial programs have investment structures aligned with LGU budgets. Contact us and we'll design a partnership that works for your situation.
Is BarangayAI aligned with DICT and DILG digital governance programs?
+
Yes. Our programs are designed to complement and support national digital governance mandates, including the eGov SuperApp initiative and DICT's digital literacy programs. We provide the compliance documentation LGUs need for national reporting requirements.
What if our barangay is in a remote area with limited internet?
+
The BarangayAI OS is optimized for low-bandwidth environments and includes offline-capable learning modules for areas with intermittent connectivity. We also work with community internet access points, school computer labs, and shared devices where individual smartphone access is limited.
How does BarangayAI protect the privacy of our residents?
+
Full compliance with the Philippine Data Privacy Act (RA 10173) is built into everything we do. Resident data is never sold to third parties. We are transparent about how all data is collected, used, and stored — and we provide barangay officials with full control over their community's data. See our Privacy Policy for details.
How long does it take to deploy BarangayAI in our community?
+
A Pilot Barangay deployment typically takes 2–4 weeks from signed partnership agreement to launch — including platform setup, staff orientation, and the first community training session. Municipal and provincial rollouts follow a structured timeline agreed upon with your LGU during the assessment phase.
